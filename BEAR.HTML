Sloth Bear

The sloth bear is a myrmecophagous bear species native to the Indian subcontinent. It feeds on fruits, ants and termites. It is listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List, mainly because of habitat loss and degradation. It has also been called "labiated bear" because of its long lower lip and palate used for sucking insects. It has a long, shaggy fur, a mane around the face, and long, sickle-shaped claws. It is lankier than brown and Asian black bears. It shares features of insectivorous mammals and evolved during the Pleistocene from the ancestral brown bear through convergent evolution.

  • Name: Melursus ursinus
  • Family: Ursidae
  • Total Population: 20,000
  • Kingdom: Indian Subcontinent
  • Habitat: In areas with thick forest cover and low hills.
  • National Parks: Shoolpaneshwar, Ratanmahal, Jessore, and Balaram Ambaji Sanctuaries.
  • Status in the Wild: Vulnerable
